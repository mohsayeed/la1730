Impact on Public and Society

The mass media in India is playing a critical role in distributing COVID-related information to the general people and encouraging them to follow specific safe procedures.

Social media became an important place to interact during a period of social distance and restricted contact with others. Social media platforms are intended to bring people together, and they have greatly aided in keeping the world linked during the pandemic.

Since many individuals have been ordered to stay at home, they have resorted to social media to keep in touch with friends and family and to pass the time. The time spent on mass media increased during lockdown has increase significantly.

Social media has been used by news outlets, organisations, and the general public to spread both valid information and misinformation about the pandemic. The influence of social media panic on people varies according on the gender, age, and degree of education of the individual.

The COVID-19 pandemic has had an impact on how the ordinary public, celebrities, world leaders, and professionals use social media. Social networking sites have been utilised to distribute information as well as provide entertainment and distraction from the pandemic.

Positive Impact

The internet can be used to distribute a public awareness campaign designed to educate adults on helpful skills that can assist children and teenagers cope with the pandemic's problems

The CDC, WHO, medical journals, and health care organisations have been updating and spreading information across numerous platforms with partnerships with Facebook, Google Scholar, TikTok. Based upon the information and directions being distributed through the media, frontline workers are working diligently, and government agencies are more active.

During a pandemic, the mass media is a potent instrument for halting disease spread, and it has a significant impact on people's behaviour

Negative Impact on Public and Society

People can stay safe, informed, and connected thanks to technological improvements and social media. The same instruments, on the other hand, enable and amplify the current infodemic, which continues to undermine global response and risk pandemic control measures.

The rapid spread of the coronavirus pandemic has many businesses shut down and many workers either out of work or working from home.

The spread of misinformation, aided by social media and other digital platforms, has proven to be a greater threat to global public health than the virus itself, as the COVID-19 pandemic has shown. Exaggeration and misunderstanding of COVID-related news were spread by TV news and social media, causing alarm among the general people.

Many platforms struggled to filter what was uploaded and shared quickly enough to spread of false information.

This was owing to an increase in AI use, since many human moderators were sent home during shelter in place orders and faced contract constraints, making it impossible for them to continue working from home This approach failed to limit the spread of COVID-19 falsehoods and also removed other useful material and links to articles.
